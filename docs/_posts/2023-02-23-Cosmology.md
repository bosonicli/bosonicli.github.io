# Friedmann Equation

$$
\begin{aligned}
    H & \equiv \frac{\dot{a}}{a}    \\
    H^2 &= ( \frac{\dot{a}}{a} )^2    \\
    &= \frac{ 8 \pi G \rho + \Lambda c^2 }{3} - \frac{ k c^2 }{a^2}  \\
    \dot{H} + H^2 &= \frac{\ddot{a}}{a} \\
    &= - \frac{ 4 \pi G }{3} (\rho + \frac{3p}{c^2}) + \frac{ \Lambda c^2 }{3}  \\
    R &= \frac{6}{c^2} ( \frac{\ddot{a}}{a} + ( \frac{\dot{a}}{a} )^2 + \frac{ k c^2 }{a^2} )
\end{aligned}
$$
